Assessing Dysarthric Speech Intelligibility via Generative Augmentation and Self-Supervised Linguistic Representations

Davide Lillini, Carlo Aironi, Lucia Migliorelli, Leonardo Gabrielli and Stefano Squartini

Abstract

The development of robust automated systems for assessing speech intelligibility levels in patients with dysarthria is currently hindered by the scarcity of labeled pathological datasets. This study proposes a novel framework addressing this limitation by integrating self-supervised learning with generative data augmentation. We employ Wav2Vec 2.0, a pre-trained self- supervised speech model, to extract and analyze contextualized representations, aligned with both character and phoneme tar- gets, systematically identifying which linguistic units serve as the most effective biomarkers for binary intelligibility classification. To address data scarcity, we employ StyleTTS-2, a diffusion-based generative model, to generate synthetic dysarthric speech samples conditioned on reference audio from the TORGO dataset and text prompts from the UA-Speech dataset. Experimental results demonstrate that contextualized representations yield superior classification accuracy compared to approaches operating on full word- or utterance-level audio. Significantly higher performance is observed on units most degraded by dysarthric impairment, validating the proposed fine-grained approach. Furthermore, synthetic data augmentation proved to enhance model robustness, effectively mitigating data scarcity issues.
