Enhancing Speech Intelligibility Assessment in Dysarthria via Generative Speech Augmentation and Acoustic Modelling

Authors: Davide Lillini, Carlo Aironi, Lucia Migliorelli, Leonardo Gabrielli and Stefano Squartini

Abstract

The development of robust automated systems for dysarthria assessment is currently hindered by the scarcity of high-quality labelled pathological datasets. This study proposes a novel framework to address this limitation by integrating Self-Supervised Learning with generative data augmentation. The methodology employs the Wav2Vec 2.0 architecture to extract acoustic features and the StyleTTS 2 model to generate synthetic dysarthric speech samples, conditioned on reference audio from the TORGO database and text prompts from the UA-Speech corpus. A siamese MLP classifier was applied to perform a binary classification of intelligibility levels (Low versus High) under a strict speaker-independent protocol. Experimental results demonstrate that the inclusion of synthetic data systematically improves classification performance across various linguistic units. To quantify the impact of the generated samples relative to the data volume increase, an Augmentation Efficiency Index (AEI) was introduced. The findings confirm that the proposed pipeline effectively mitigates data scarcity issues, with the AEI revealing that specific under-represented units benefit most efficiently from the synthetic integration.
